2021 - Year in Review

October 31, 2021

Over the last year, I have realized that living a more fulfilling life is mostly about getting the basics right. Having enough money to never have to worry about money, being healthy, being social, and working on things you care about.

I know it sounds silly, but prior to this, I had a different idea of how I want to live life. I always had this itch that I have to do something great or special. But looking back, the happiest moments in my life have always been the ones that cover my most fundamental needs as a human being. Anything else is overcompensating for a deficit.

I now feel that you can have a balanced life, and at the same time you can avoid a mediocre life. If you earn a lot of money and you are still productive, healthy, loving, and at peace, then you have struck the right balance.

Earlier this year, I went to Switzerland for an internship. I stayed there for 8 months. It was both good and bad. Things were not so good in the beginning, but they were better after the pandemic measures loosened. One thing that was apparent was that the people there were happy.

It was the first time in a long time when I was yanked out of the autopilot that I was going through. I got a glimpse at the pinnacle of what life could be like. It taught me a lot.

This was in sharp contrast to how I spent my time in 2020, when I was at home due to lockdown and I became extremely lazy. A big reason why I decided to go to Switzerland was because I became aware of how complacent I was getting, and I wanted to get out of that rut.

Getting into uncomfortable situations is key. They allow us to fail more, and consequently learn more. This is what happened to me over the last year and I am a much better person because of it.

> Your comfort zone is killing you.

I picked up Siddhartha while I was in Amsterdam on a trip. This book influenced me a lot. Earlier, I used to micromanage my productivity. Now I have realized that I don't need to be "#productive" I just need to keep doing stuff as it comes.

Though I loved Atomic Habits, it seems that such self-help or drive-by advice doesn't work out in the long term. Sure, there were some lessons I liked that I might have internalized without knowing. But ultimately, I think you need to experience life as it comes, and learn from your mistakes.

If I tend to be too preachy in my writings, you don't have to follow what I write. I am only writing for myself, as it fits my life. Not yours. Go do stuff, fail. Fail often, learn often.

In our education system, the importance of "direction" is quite overstated. It took me a while to unlearn that. And I still find myself going back looking for advice, or shiny new stuff to do. When in reality, I know that the best thing is to be consistent on the boring old fundamentals.

Recently, I have been frequently prompting myself "What is it that I truly desire in life?". Last year I made a lot of promises on things I'd like to improve on. But now I feel like I should go back to first asking what I truly want.

This is a question that we need to keep asking ourselves all the time. Our wants change with time, and sometimes that change doesn't even surface to our conscious thoughts.

> What is it that I truly want?

I'm not entirely sure, but I want something to do with getting the basics right.

Also, what I don't want is to live a parasitic life. I want to live my life with intention. To not just let life pass me by. To not live on autopilot.

So... what now?

As far as work is concerned, I'm still in computer security and I feel that there are enough opportunities out there for me. I'm glad I decided to stick to something instead of jumping around.

To be honest, I didn't focus much on the other things I had promised I would work on. I stagnated. I didn't work on eating better, or exercising more. Need to focus more in this area.

But I would say, I'm generally much happier these days as compared to last year. I feel like I'm in control, and I feel grounded and at peace. Getting out of your comfort zone eventually makes you more comfortable in your own skin.
