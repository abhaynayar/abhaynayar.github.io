2021 - Year in Review

October 31, 2021

Over the last year, I have realized that living a more fulfilling life is mostly about getting the basics right. Having enough money to never have to worry about money, being healthy, being social, and working on things you care about.

I know it sounds silly, but prior to this, I had a different idea of how I wanted to live life. I always had this itch that I have to do something great or special. But looking back, the happiest moments in my life have always been the ones that covered my most fundamental needs as a human being. Anything else was overcompensation for a deficit.

Earlier this year, I went to Switzerland for an internship. I stayed there for 8 months. It was both good and bad. Things were not so good in the beginning, but they were better after the pandemic measures loosened. Despite all the hardships brought on by the circumstances, I did get to learn a lot while I was there.

It was the first time in a long time that I was yanked out of the autopilot that I was going through. I got a glimpse at the pinnacle of what life could be like.

This was in sharp contrast to how I spent my time in 2020. I was at home due to lockdown, and I became extremely lazy. A big reason why I decided to go to Switzerland was because I became aware of how complacent I was getting, and I wanted to get out of that rut.

Getting into uncomfortable situations is key. They allow us to fail more, and consequently learn more. This is what happened to me over the last year and I am a much better person because of it.

> Your comfort zone is killing you.

I picked up Siddhartha while I was in Amsterdam on a trip. This book influenced me a lot. Earlier, I used to micromanage my productivity. Now I just do stuff as it comes.

Though I loved Atomic Habits, I think such self-help or drive-by advice doesn't work in the long term. Sure, there were some lessons I liked that I might have internalized without knowing. But ultimately, I think you need to experience life as it comes, and learn from your mistakes.

In the same vein, if I tend to be too preachy in my writings, you don't have to follow what I write. I am only writing for myself as it fits my life, not yours. Go do stuff. Fail. Fail often, learn often.

In our education system, the importance of direction is quite overstated. It took me quite a while to unlearn that. Still I find myself going back, looking for advice, or shiny new stuff to do, when I know that the best thing to do is to be consistent on the boring old fundamentals.

Recently, I have been asking myself, "What is it that I truly desire in life?". Last year I made a lot of promises on things I'd like to improve on. But I feel like I should go back to first asking what I truly want.

This is a question that we need to keep asking ourselves all the time. Our wants change with time, and sometimes that change doesn't surface to our conscious thoughts.

> What is it that I truly want?

I'm not entirely sure. But I do want to get the basics right. What I don't want is to live a parasitic life. I want to live my life with intention, not on autopilot.

So... what now?

As far as work is concerned, I'm still in computer security and I feel that there are enough opportunities for me out there. I love my job, and I am constantly working on getting better at my skills in my own time.

However, I didn't focus much on the other things I had promised I would work on. I stagnated. I didn't work on eating better, or exercising more. Need to focus more in this area.

I'm much happier these days compared to the last year. I feel grounded, and at peace. Getting out of my comfort zone has made me much more comfortable in my own skin.
