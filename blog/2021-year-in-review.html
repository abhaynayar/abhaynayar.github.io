2021 - Year in Review

October 31, 2021

Over the last year, I have realized that living a more fulfilling life is mostly about getting the basics right. Being financially stable, being healthy, being social, and working on things you care about.

Looking back, the happiest moments in my life have always been the ones where my most fundamental needs as a human being were fulfilled. Anything else was overcompensation for a deficit.

Your comfort zone is killing you

Earlier this year, I went to Switzerland for an internship for 8 months. It was both good and bad. Things were not so good in the beginning, but they were better after the pandemic measures loosened. Despite all the hardships brought on by the circumstances, I did get to learn a lot while I was there.

It was the first time in a long time that I was yanked out of the autopilot that I was going through. I got a glimpse at the pinnacle of what life could be like.

This was in sharp contrast to how I spent my time in 2020. I was at home due to lockdown, and I was put through some really hard times which left me shattered. A big reason why I decided to go to Switzerland was because I wanted to get out of it. I didn't want to live my life passively.

Over the last year I got out of my comfort zone a lot more. I failed at things a lot more, and consequently I learnt a lot more. I am a much better person because of it. I only wish I had been thrown into the water earlier.

> Your comfort zone is killing you.

What is it that you truly desire in life?

Recently, I have been asking myself, "What is it that I truly desire in life?". Last year I made a lot of promises on things I wanted to improve upon. But I feel like I should first go back to asking what I truly want. Our wants change with time, and sometimes that change doesn't bubble up to our surface-level thoughts.

> What is it that I truly want?

I'm not entirely sure. But most importantly, I want to fulfil my basic needs first. Then I want to work on things where I actually love the process and not just the end result.

In our education system, the importance of direction is quite overstated. It took me quite a while to unlearn that. Sometimes, I still find myself looking for advice, or shiny new stuff to do, when I know that the best thing to do is to be consistent on the process and to not worry about the "goals".

> "The score takes care of itself"

So... what now?

As far as work is concerned, I'm still in computer security and I feel that there are enough opportunities for me out there. I love my job, and I think security is an interesting enough field for me to continue working on.

In my 2019 blog post, I had mentioned how much of an impact Atomic Habits had on me. I had left those ideas to dust for a while, but I've recently picked them back up. Going forward, I will be taking small steps, consistently.
