2022 - Year in Review

November 18, 2022

This year, I had a realization that spurred a very fundamental shift in my attitude towards what to pursue in life.

If you would have asked me before about what I really wanted in life, I would have said something on the lines of — getting really good at some niche technical field. This is why I spent most of my schooling years getting really good at computers — because I genuinely liked it.

Of course I am still grateful for having a passion in something that is easy to build a career in, most people never get to do that. But over the last year or two, I have come to the conclusion that certain other things have a much higher return on my happiness in general.

Memento mori is Latin for remember death.

I had come across the concept of memento mori a few times before in my life, but I always felt that thinking about my death would only make me anxious, so I ignored the concept of memento mori whenever I came across it. I had the typical lame answer of "I'm not afraid of death."

Eventually, I realized that people who are not afraid of death, aren't grateful of how precious life is. That is when I realized that having the fear of death is actually a good thing. Death has a lot to teach us about how to live and spend our time.

So I made a life in weeks chart for myself. In this chart, you can visualize how much of your life you have already lived, if you live up to 80 years. So there are 80 rows in total, each consisting of 52 cells. Each week, one cell fills up automatically. Indicating your slow march towards death.

Of course you don't have to be living on the edge every single moment. That is the kind of thinking that drives most people away from memento mori. Instead, you have to focus on one or two things at a time to improve on, and then realize that most worthwhile change happens over a long enough time horizon.

So in a way, memento mori only sets your priorities straight, you still have to work towards them. It forces you to be honest with yourself about what you truly want, and makes you realize that you don't have time to waste, on being too afraid to pursue what you care about.

To be honest with myself, I listened more closely to my body and my mind over the last year or so. After constantly asking myself, "is this what makes me happy?", there appeared to be only two things that contributed most towards my happiness — health and relationships.

So that's what I have been focusing on since. Focusing on things that contribute towards my physical health, and focusing on increasing my social interaction surface area. Of course, these things are not easy and they take time. But looking at my life chart filling up every week, prevents me from getting complacent.

Until next time.
