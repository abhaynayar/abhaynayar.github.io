2022 - Year in Review

December 30, 2022

If you would have asked me before about what I really wanted in life, I would have said something on the lines of "I want to be really skilled in some niche technical field." That is why I spent a lot of my time getting really good at computers — because I genuinely liked it.

Of course I am still very grateful for being able to work in a field that I'm interested in. But over the last year or two, I gradually started becoming more honest with my self about what I truly wanted in life. That's when I realized that my self was misaligned with how I had actually been living.

I came across the concept of memento mori this year. Although I had come across it a few times before, I always felt that thinking about my death would only make me anxious, so I used to ignore it.

Eventually, I realized that having the fear of death is actually a good thing. Death has a lot to teach us about how to live and spend our time. It forces us to be honest with ourselves. And it makes us realize that we don't have enough time, to waste on doing things that are not essential for our happiness.

I also made a life in weeks chart as a reminder to myself. Every row in the chart represents one year. Within each row, there are 52 cells representing the weeks. As each week of my life finishes, one cell gets filled up.

Of course you don't need to be living on the edge every single moment. Instead, it's better to focus on a few things at a time to improve upon, and then realize that most worthwhile change happens over a long enough time horizon. But in any case, once you are aligned within, the journey itself becomes the reward. You become solution-oriented, and outcome-independent.

After asking myself, "is this what makes me happy?" every now and then over the last year or so, eventually, over a long period of time, I arrived at a set of things that I now know to be true to my self. These things are unique to every person, and every person has to figure out their own priorities for themselves. This can't be taught, it can only be learnt through living.

To anyone reading this, I hope that you realize (as soon as possible) how precious life is, and start working towards making the most of your time here on earth. Have an amazing year, and an amazing life ahead.
