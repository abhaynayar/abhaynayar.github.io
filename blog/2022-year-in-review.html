2022 - Year in Review

December 30, 2022

If you would have asked me before about what I really wanted in life, I would have said something on the lines of "I want to be really skilled in some niche technical field." That is why I spent a lot of my time getting really good at computers — because I genuinely liked it.

I came across the concept of memento mori earlier this year. It is Latin for remember death. I had come across it a few times before, but I had always felt that thinking about my death would only make me anxious, so I used to ignore it.

Eventually, I realized that being aware of death is actually a good thing. Death has a lot to teach us about how to live and spend our time. It forces us to be honest with ourselves, about what we truly want, and it yanks us out of living life on autopilot.

I also made a life in weeks chart as a reminder to myself. Every row in the chart represents one year. Within each row, there are 52 cells representing the weeks. As each week of my life passes, one cell gets filled up.

Remembering that you're going to die doesn't mean you quit your job and go on a vacation. Instead, it's a catalyst towards making you prioritise what you want in life and working towards getting it.

Most worthwhile change requires consistent effort over a long period of time, so it's important to keep doing things without expecting anything in return. The universe doesn't owe anyone anything.

Over the last year I started thinking about what things contributed the most towards my happiness. And I realized that I had spent way too much time trying to be great at certain random things that I was interested in, instead of getting good enough at simpler but more essential contributors towards my happiness.

I now believe in separating my work from my identity. I think it's important to be professional at work and have a great work ethic, but as far as interests go, I think it's best if they're distanced from monetization.

Apart from that, I came up with three things that I realized to be essential for my happiness — health, wealth, and relationships. None of these are required in abundance, but you realize their importance when you're starved for any one of them.

Out of these three things, I'm okay in two, and weak in one. So I have been working on the one that I'm weak in, and trying to maintain status quo on the rest of the two. I will continue to do that for now.

So that's what I have been prioritizing this year. Of course, change is not easy, and things take time - especially given that I have been neglecting some of these things until recently. But looking at my life chart filling up every week prevents me from losing track of my priorities.

To anyone reading this, I hope that you realize how precious your time here on earth is, and I hope that you are working towards making the most of it. Have an amazing year, and an amazing life ahead.
